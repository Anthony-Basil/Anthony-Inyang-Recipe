My Favorite Chocolate Chip Cookies : Anthony Inyang

Imagine a small, round, golden-brown soft disc. With a soft, smooth surface. And lumpy texture. What about the scattered, darker-colored pieces of chocolate—the chocolate chips. Some of these are whole, and some are melted into gooey, glistening pools, giving the cookie a distinct, speckled appearance. The edges are usually a slightly darker brown, hinting at a crispy bite, while the center looks softer and chewier.

Recipe Information

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks or 226g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup (165g) packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 1/4 cups (270g) all-purpose flour

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Cream the butter and sugars, then beat in the eggs and vanilla until smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the dry ingredients, then mix them into the wet ingredients.
  4. Stir in the chocolate chips and drop spoonfuls of the dough onto the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 9-11 minutes until golden, then let cool for a few minutes before moving to a wire rack.
